Quantum Machine Learning and Grover’s Algorithm for robot optimization

Optimizing high-degree-of-freedom robotic manipulators requires searching complex, high-dimensional configuration spaces. This work introduces a quantum-native framework integrating quantum machine learning with Grover’s algorithm for efficient kinematic optimization.

A Modified Lie Group-Based Synthesis for Parallel Mechanisms: Addressing Uncontrollable and Immovable Limb Cases

The enhanced method integrates passive degrees of freedom and immovable limb effects into the synthesis process, improving motion representation for mechanisms where conventional intersection operations can fail.

Parallel robot balancing

A Parallel Robot for Dynamic Balancing

The project shows a three-limb R–R–S parallel manipulator performing high-precision tip–tilt balancing using a constraint-consistent 6×6 Jacobian and passive joint angle estimation instead of direct platform pose sensing.

  • Three identical R–R–S limbs, 120° apart.
  • Controlled Rx–Ry tip–tilt with constrained Tx, Ty, and Rz.
  • Jerk-limited trajectories, Jacobian synchronization, and ball-position feedback.
